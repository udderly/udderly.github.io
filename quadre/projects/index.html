Music with a Message

Quadre’s Music with a Message project shines a spotlight on issues that concern all of us today. Through new commissions by local and international composers, the ensemble partners with local 501(c)(3) nonprofit social organizations and collaborates with professors at Stanford and the University of California, Berkeley.

Since Quadre’s beginnings in the ’90s, the group has sought out ways to connect with the community at civic functions, youth and senior centers, and schools. This spirit has guided the group over the years towards a greater emphasis on social issues.

The Music with a Message project provides Quadre a platform to serve as a catalyst and vehicle for social change. Our duty as interpreters of society’s consciousness is to create programs for our audiences that engender discussion and action on issues such as homelessness; climate change and the environment; social, gender and racial inequality; and over-consumption.

For the 2020–2021 season, Quadre will be working with a local homeless shelter, food pantry, youth center, women’s advocacy group, school for the deaf, prison, and low-cost housing facility around these issues.

Quadre announces its 2020 composition contest. With two composers in the group, Daniel Wood and Nathan Pawelek, the ensemble has a keen interest in supporting the work of other composers and expanding the repertoire for horn quartet.

2020 Quadre Composition Contest – New Music for Horn

The Submission Deadline has passed. Winners will be announced by June 4, 2020.

As part of Quadre’s mission, the ensemble announces a composition contest to stimulate interest in writing for the horn and provide an avenue for horn works to be performed. Two awards will be given. First Prize $500, Second Prize $300.

Competition Details

  1. Works may be for horn quartet or horn quartet + solo percussion. No electronica this year. The work can be a single or multi-movement piece. For works with percussion, any standard non-pitched percussion can be used that can be performed by one player.
  2. All composers are eligible. There is no fee.
  3. Please submit only one piece 6 to 12 minutes in duration. Include a PDF of your score and a set of performance parts. Parts and scores should be fully edited and easily readable. Manuscript size will not exceed 8.5″ x 14″.
  4. Submit a recording as an MP3 or MP4. Live or electronic recordings are fine.
  5. Works may have been performed before, but no published or commissioned pieces are eligible.
  6. Your name must not appear anywhere on the music. Include the work’s previous performance history, if any.
  7. The judges reserve the right to withhold prizes if in their judgment no deserving works are submitted.
  8. The decision of the contest jury will be final.
  9. All performance, copyright, and royalty rights of all the works submitted remain with the composers.
  10. Please send any questions to info@quadre.org.

Awards

In addition to the prize money, winning works will be performed in public during the 2020–2021 Quadre season. Winning composers will receive a program, a recording, and a written evaluation by the judging committee. Winners will be invited to attend the concert(s) for free, but no additional expenses will be reimbursed. Winning composers may also be approached for publication of their work by Solid Wood Publishing.
