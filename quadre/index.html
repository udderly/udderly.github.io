About

Described as the ensemble that “zaps the myth that classical stuff’s stuffy”, Quadre connects with their audiences everywhere, from concert halls and music festivals to soup kitchens and school auditoriums. With their vibrant music and artistic warmth, this innovative ensemble is well recognized not only for its virtuosic French horn mastery, but also for its humorous element of surprise. Their fresh and intellectually charming works make audiences resoundingly agree that experiencing a Quadre concert is a listening adventure not be missed as their music and message touches the heart and resonates deeply with the soul.

Selected by the National Endowment for the Arts and Chamber Music America as one of only a handful of chamber music ensembles for a rural residency, Quadre began its professional calling in Alabama in 1999. As musical ambassadors, Quadre traveled throughout the state, reaching citizens of all ages and backgrounds to make serious “classical” music accessible, meaningful and just plain fun. Following their successful immersion in Southern society and hospitality, the group returned to California to record the first of their four albums, titled The Voice of Four Horns.

In the course of releasing their subsequent three albums (Citrus, Our Time and Horns for the Holidays), Quadre actively commissions new works for the horn quartet genre. The ensemble draws on the compositional talents of members Nathan Pawelek and Daniel Wood as well as renowned composers David Garner, Mark Adam Watkins and Christopher Wiggins.

Quadre is equally dedicated to collaborating with other musicians, ensembles and composers to further the creation of innovative pieces, such as their work with Grammy award-winning flutist, Molly Barth; Los Angeles studio legend, James Thatcher; and Philadelphia phenom, soprano Elizabeth Weigle.

In over 1000 performances, Quadre has been featured as artists and lecturers with the Manhattan School of Music; Music Academy of the West; the 2008, 2011 and 2013 International Horn Conferences in California, Colorado and Tennessee respectively; and the Alabama Institute for Education in the Arts. Communities from Grand Forks, North Dakota to San Jose, California have invited Quadre to be concerto soloists. The group is honored to have performed for the Helen Keller School for the Deaf and Blind and the Weingarten Children’s Center for the Deaf. In many ways, these experiences remind the ensemble how the connection to the arts is vital to everyone.

Quadre keenly embraces its leadership role as music ambassadors into the hearts of the communities where the ensemble performs and visits. Equally at home in schools, senior settings and universities, Quadre’s interactive, interdisciplinary and lively community engagement programs have reached over 50,000 people across the United States. In 2000 and 2002, the San Francisco Symphony selected Quadre to participate in its unique educational outreach program, Adventures in Music. Performing for every 1st and 2nd grader in San Francisco, Quadre created engaging shows that captivated the young audiences from the start. The group creatively incorporates music, choreography and drama for wonderfully positive musical experiences that stimulate the senses and make lasting impressions upon audiences.

Our Story

“Wouldn’t it be nice if…

Daniel sent letters with these words to horn players all across Los Angeles. Twenty replies were received and after a few weeks of rehearsals, four French horn players committed to establishing Quadre – The Voice of Four Horns.

From the start, Quadre’s sound emanated from music and ideas beyond the traditional French horn experience. Our perpetually buoyant founder, Daniel, caught a bug when he watched in youthful awe, the off-beat musical “Forever Plaid.” That propelled him into the world of what he describes as “solo boy bands” (an oxymoron he explains – “They were four guys in a band but together they created one singular voice”) of the 50s and 60s, like the Hi-Lo’s and the Four Freshmen.

Quadre’s members are Amy Jo, Lydia, Nate and Daniel. Nate and his cerebral self-deprecating disposition. Lydia and her cool, sophisticated demeanor. Amy Jo and her grounded view of the world. And the driving force behind the group, Daniel and his quirky, yet passionate wit.

We continue to do what chamber groups do: record albums, tour, collaborate with other artists & ensembles, commission new work, and engage in educational and community programs. But what drives us onwards is our vision: to create and perform music as a means to enact transformable change. We don’t need to play at the most prestigious or biggest concert halls to accomplish this. We just need to touch one person at a time.

Members

Amy Jo Rhine

Amy Jo Rhine is a seasoned orchestral performer who has held principal positions with the Colorado Music Festival since 1997 and the Memphis-based IRIS Orchestra since 2001. She currently is the third horn with the Los Angeles Philharmonic where her husband, Greg Roosa is second horn. Rhine is active in the Los Angeles recording studios and can be heard on some of Hollywood’s latest blockbuster releases. She collaborated with famed studio horn player, James Thatcher on his album, Now Playing.

Prior to her family’s move to sunny SoCal, they spent 9 years in St Louis, MO where she enjoyed a diverse professional life teaching from her home private studio, held the adjunct faculty positions at both Webster and Maryville Universities and played regularly with the St Louis Symphony. A chamber music enthusiast, she has performed throughout the United States, Europe, Japan and Panama with the Lieurance Wind Quintet, the Wichita Brass Quintet, the Wilson Wind Quintet and Quadre. Rhine joined Quadre in 2008. Rhine plays a Geyer model horn by Karl Hill. Amidst busy performance schedules, Amy and Greg reserve ample time to discover the finer points of Supercars and Super Heroes with their sons, Norty and Sutton.

Lydia Van Dreel

Lydia Van Dreel, Professor of Horn at the University of Oregon, has a varied career as an orchestral, solo, chamber and recording artist. Currently, Lydia is a member of the Oregon Bach Festival Orchestra, The IRIS Orchestra (Memphis, TN), Quadre, the Eugene Symphony Orchestra, Orchestra Next, the Oregon Wind Quintet, and the Oregon Brass Quintet. Previously, Van Dreel performed as a member of the Colorado Music Festival Orchestra, the Britt Music Festival Orchestra, The Spoleto Festival Orchestra, The New World Symphony Orchestra, The Florida Wind Quintet, The Madison Symphony Orchestra, and the Oregon Mozart Players. Prior to joining the faculty at the University of Oregon, Van Dreel held a ten-year tenured position as Co-Principal Horn of the Sarasota Orchestra in Sarasota, Florida.

Professor Van Dreel collaborates on a wide variety of chamber music and recording projects. Notable recent projects include their first solo CD, “New Millennium Music for Horn”, released in 2014 on the Quadre Records Label, “Red Desert”, music of David Crumb, released on the BRIDGE record label in 2015, a featured performance in the 2012 Superbowl Chrysler commercial “It’s Halftime in America”, and a world premier performance at the 47th annual International Horn Symposium in Los Angeles of “Gjallarhorn”, an electro-acoustic piece for natural horn and electronics controlled by motion detector, created in collaboration with composer, inventor and intermedia technology instructor, Chester Udell.

Nathan Pawelek

Nathan has worked with nationally-recognized artists, such as Aretha Franklin and Arlo Guthrie, and international stars: Gil Shahan, Joshua Bell, YoYo Ma, Midori, and Leonard Slatkin. Nathan attended the Tanglewood Music Festival and performed with Seiji Ozawa. He was a participant in the European Mozart Academy in Poland, The National Repertory Orchestra in Breckenridge, CO, The Norfolk Chamber Music Festival, and he played under Michael Tilson-Thomas with the New World Symphony. Nathan was a former member of the horn section of the Colorado Music Festival Orchestra in Boulder. He joined Quadre in 2003.

Nathan attended Oberlin College and Conservatory where he received double degrees in horn performance and English. He didn’t trust that a career focused solely on music would be the most fulfilling or practical. He went on to earn a Master of Horn Performance from Yale University and then a Master of Fine Arts in creative writing from Goddard College in Vermont. His wife is Karen and his children are Aidan and Zachary, and they live in Guilford, CT, a sleepy coastal town on Long Island Sound. He has a dog named Clara who is large and looks like a polar bear. Nathan enjoys a gluten-free diet and misses wheat-laden chocolate chip cookies.

Daniel Wood

A multi-faceted artist, Daniel has written and performed improvisational solo shows for the horn, piano, and electronics. After his residency with Quadre in 1999 – 2000 in Alabama, Daniel became deeply drawn to the issues of access to the arts, the environment, and society’s insatiable consumption of products. Examining the theme of music and diversity in the United States, Daniel premiered his show, “Life on an Island,” followed with his show, “Brother, Can You Spare a Dime,” a depression-era musical retrospective. More recently, Daniel’s “East meets West” addressed international collaboration between American jazz artists and improvisatory Iranian Persian musicians.

Compositionally, Daniel’s works favor intimate groups in the classical and jazz mediums. His pieces evoke a sense of time and place, leading listeners to reflect, cherish, and oftentimes, smile. In addition to his many works for Quadre, notable commissions include “Our Hot Date with Destiny” for wind quintet from Avenue Winds, “Space Available” for horn duet from musicians of the Los Angeles Philharmonic and “Blinding Light” from the Presidio String Trio.

An engaging performer, composer, and educator, Daniel founded Quadre in 1998. He plays a Ricco-Kuhn horn. When not delving into the latest musical project, he enjoys spending time with his wife and three kids on hikes, bike rides and trips to the beach and beyond.

Our Mission

Education

Quadre’s core mission to make music more accessible extends far beyond the concert hall. Equally at home in K-12 classrooms and colleges or universities, our interactive interdisciplinary outreach activities have reached over 50,000 people across the United States. Quadre’s nationwide education residencies in urban and rural environments have been supported by funding from prominent organizations including, The National Endowment for the Arts, Chamber Music America, The San Francisco Symphony and The Arts Council of Silicon Valley.

Any aspect of the following educational initiatives can be combined to create a unique multi-day residency in your community or school.

Community Engagement

We strive to serve the communities where we live and are dedicated to engagement with the community. We recognize that this need exists throughout the nation and seek to serve every community we visit in the same manner. The purpose of our engagement is to reach out to underserved populations and the community at large. Venues for such events inclue, but are not limited to: Title IV schools, hospitals, eldercare facilities, hospices, homes for troubled youth, prisons, shopping ceners, train stations, downtown areas and sporting venues.

Commisions, Collaborations & Competitions

As our universal culture becomes more rich and diverse, we strive to make our presentations reflect the attitudes and desires of our listeners. Quadre believes that the creation and promotion of new music nurtures composers and opens the minds of young performers to new music that is essential to this cause. As we collaborate with artists of all disciplines, the synthesis of joint programs and presentations can add diversity to our own artistic expression and create new avenues to explore, which are vital to the artistic process.

Albums

Our Time, Quadre’s newest CD release, explores the intricate human condition through relationships with time and change. The culmination of a two-year journey to commission new composers and to collaborate with non horn-playing instrumental artists, the pieces reflect on life’s profound moments of love, longing, aging and awakening. Grammy Award winning flutist Molly Barth, percussionist James Kassis and soprano Elizabeth Weigle join Quadre on the CD performing new music by Brian Holmes, Nathan Pawelek, Mark Carlson, Daniel Wood and Jamie Keesecker.

Horns for the Holidays features original holiday arrangements from Quadre in collaboration with Hollywood superstar horn player, James Thatcher, percussionist James Kassis, and the Bellfire handbell choir. Nestle by the fireplace and enjoy these renditions that sparkle with the holiday spirit.

Citrus features all original brass music influenced by jazz and world dance music. Quadre brings to life works by David Garner, Christopher Wiggins, Mark Adam Watkins, Nathan Pawelek and Daniel Wood with the nuance of a group that has come into its own. Listeners are treated to a feast of pieces filled with energy and vibe – the spirit of dancing the night away with a waltz, lander and conga.

The Voice of Four Horns, Quadre’s inaugural CD features classical repertoire for horn quartet, popular arrangements and original music commissioned by the ensemble. Warm, playful and agile – Quadre’s distinct sound shines through. Listen and wander through this bright and lively musical excursion.
