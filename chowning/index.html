Emphasis on Technique

Philosophy


Utilizing the Bel Canto technique, students learn to sing with less strain to their vocal folds and develop healthy singing habits. The benefits are enourmous.

As students progress, they gain confidence and develop artistic refinement for a lifetime of singing pleasure and performance skills. Children who begin with the Bel Canto method, rather than singing pop style, particularly benefit and avoid permanently damaging their young, growing voices.


As far as is known, the art and science of vocal pedagogy began in the Catholic Churches of Spain in the 14th century, spread to Italy, and evolved by the early 17th century into a style of singing known as “Bel Canto”.

It is not commonly known that Bel Canto, “beautiful singing”, is a very specific vocal technique. Schools were dedicated to developing the Bel Canto technique carried out by master vocal teachers, while many of whom often maintained successful composing and singing careers.

These early teachers were as interested in the study of voice production, the vocal apparatus and its natural physical behavior as they were in music and artful singing. They wrote their findings in treatises, which found their way to the libraries throughout Europe.

With the use of computers and other advanced research tools, researchers are discovering that a surprisingly large amount of the Bel Canto theory is supported by contemporary science.

I believe in the importance of such ongoing research to vocal teaching, as it informs and guides the development and application of Bel Canto technique today and its ultimate benefit for all my students.

Bel Canto Method


Beautiful Singing Method

Bel Canto is a time-honoured vocal training system which when developed allows the greatest ease in executing long, languid phrases, agility, and expansive vocal range.

Increase vocal flexibility and range, improve vocal register shifts, gain resonance and tonal warmth, and attain pitch accuracy and breath management.

Instruction


Voice Lessons

The focus of the voice lessons will be on learning techniques appropriate for each student's individuals needs. Building on the Bel Canto foundation, the student can develop a unique path of expression with the following five learning modules:

  • Breathing I & Posture
  • Vocal Placement & Resonance
  • Articulation & Breathing II
  • Practice Studies & Listening Library
  • Artistry & Performance

About


Coloratura soprano Maureen Chowning studied at the Boston and NEC conservatories before moving to the San Francisco area. She has since appeared on the Public Broadcasting Systems’ NOVA series and Smithsonian World with Max Mathews, demonstrating his Radio Baton and conductor program throughout the world.

She has performed and premiered works at the International Electronic Music Festival ar Bourges, France, where in 1990 she gave the world premiere of Solemn Songs for Evening by Richard Boulanger. In 1997, she gave the premiere of Sea Songs by Dexter Morrill, and she premiered Joanne Carey's Three Spanish Songs. In March 2005, she gave the world premiere of "Voices", composed for her by John Chowning for computer generated sound and solo soprano at the Maison de Radio in Paris and in March 2006, the US premiere, as part of a Berkeley Symphony Concert series. She has also performed "Oscura" by Jean-Claude Risset at Buenos Aires, Quito, Rome, Paris, and CCRMA, Stanford University.

She is noted for her special ability to sing comfortably in alternative tunings, such as the Pierce scale and in John Chowning's "Voices". Among other composers that she has worked with are Kirke Mechem, Qui Dong, Servio Marin, and Atau Tanaka.

Her classical repertoire includes choral and oratorio solos and opera. She has performed several coloratura operatic roles such as the Queen of the Night from Mozart's The Magic Flute. Mrs. Chowning has maintained a busy private studio for voice pedagogy for thirty years as is very active giving masterclasses around the world.
